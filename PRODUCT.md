# Handley Lab HVP Portal

register: product

## What this is
An independent, lab-authored synthesis of the NIH Human Virome Program (HVP): what it
is, who is in it, what data it is generating, and how that data flows. Maintained by
the Handley Lab (Washington University in St. Louis). It is a scholarly resource, not
an official HVP product and not a marketing site.

## Users
- Virome and microbiome researchers who want a fast, honest picture of the program.
- The Handley Lab itself, as the framework for its own analyses, reanalyses, and
  evaluations of HVP tools and data.
- Scientifically literate readers (program staff, students, interested public).

They read on desktops in daytime, scanning for specific facts and for the lab's
synthesis. They value accuracy and legibility over polish.

## Tone
Credible, sober, scholarly. Opinionated where the lab has a view, but always grounded
in cited, verifiable data. Reads like a well-made scientific document or a serious
university resource, not a startup landing page.

## Anti-references (do not resemble)
- SaaS / startup landing pages: drenched hero color, giant display type, "go big"
  choreography, feature-card grids, gradient accents.
- Biotech-brand clichés: teal-on-navy, DNA-helix decoration, hype copy.
- Anything that reads as marketing or as an official consortium product.

## Principles
- Data honesty above all. Show caveats inline (planned vs deposited; parent-biobank
  enrollment kept separate from HVP participants). Never overstate scale.
- Aggregate discipline: others' unpublished data appears only as program-level
  aggregates; per-PI specifics are withheld until public.
- Independence is stated plainly and persistently ("Handley Lab synthesis, not an
  official HVP product").
- Every figure carries provenance and an "as of" date.
- Restraint with intent: legibility and structure over decoration.
